About Kesar Mangoes

The Kesar mango boasts several distinctive features that contribute to its revered status as a premium mango variety. One of its standout characteristics is the unique saffron or golden color, which not only gives it a visually appealing allure but also distinguishes it from other mango varieties. Another specialty of the Kesar mango lies in its smooth and fiberless texture. The creamy consistency of its flesh enhances the eating experience, making it a favorite for those who appreciate a velvety and indulgent mouthfeel. The combination of its unique color, exquisite taste, and smooth texture contributes to the Kesar mango's versatility in culinary applications.

Kesar mango, renowned for its exceptional quality, is primarily cultivated in the western Indian state of Gujarat. Within Gujarat, the Gir region, specifically around the districts of Junagadh, Amreli, and Bhavnagar, is particularly renowned for producing the finest Kesar mangoes. The geographical conditions of this region, including the soil type and climate, contribute significantly to the unique characteristics of the Kesar mango. The Gir region, known for its rich agricultural landscape, provides an ideal environment for the cultivation of Kesar mangoes. The well-drained alluvial soil and the warm climate with distinct seasons create optimal conditions for the fruit to thrive. The Kesar mango orchards in this region come alive during the harvesting season, typically from April to June, producing mangoes that are not only cherished locally but also sought after globally.
